two sections after it (a) define every parameter and (b) walk through the arrows one at
a time, stating for each step its inputs → operation → output and the logic of how the
quantities combine.

How the quantities combine, step by step
Each step is one arrow in the figure.
Step 1 — hyper-prior to prior parameters (𝜂 → 𝜋, 𝜇, 𝑘). Inputs: 𝜂 . Operation:
(𝜋, 𝜇, 𝑘) = 𝑓(𝜂). Output: the three prior parameters. Logic: 𝜂 is your belief about
what the prior looks like; 𝑓 reads off its current best values for the mixing weight, centre,
and strength. If you are uncertain about 𝜂 , you instead carry the whole distribution 𝑝(𝜂)
and average over it later (Step 2, predictive form).
Step 2 — prior parameters to the prior (𝜋, 𝜇, 𝑘 → 𝑝(𝜃)). Inputs: 𝜋, 𝜇, 𝑘. Operation:

Output: the mixture prior over direction. How they combine: 𝜇 places the bump, 𝑘 sets its
width, and 𝜋 mixes it with a uniform floor — 𝜋 is the height of the bump relative to the flat

which is wider and heavier-tailed [3].
